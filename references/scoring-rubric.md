# LinkedIn Credibility Audit — Scoring Rubric

Reference for detailed score criteria on each dimension.

## 1. Profile Photo & Banner

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 1 | No photo, or photo is a logo/cartoon/landscape. Banner is default LinkedIn blue. |
| 2 | Photo exists but is low-resolution, poorly lit, cropped from a group shot, or clearly a casual selfie. Banner is irrelevant stock image. |
| 3 | Photo is clear, face visible, professional-adjacent (business casual). Banner exists but is generic. |
| 4 | Photo is high-quality, well-lit, friendly/professional expression. Banner is relevant to industry or role. |
| 5 | Photo communicates trust instantly — genuine expression, appropriate dress for target audience. Banner reinforces positioning with a clear message or value prop. |

## 2. Headline

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 1 | Just a job title or company name. No value proposition. |
| 2 | Job title + company + 1-2 buzzwords. Doesn't differentiate. |
| 3 | States what the person does, somewhat specific. May be keyword-dense but reads awkwardly. |
| 4 | Clear role + target audience + credible hook. Reads naturally. |
| 5 | In 220 characters, tells the reader: who this is for, what problem they solve, and why they're credible. Memorable phrasing. |

## 3. About Section

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 1 | Empty or a single sentence ("Experienced professional in..." ). |
| 2 | Generic paragraph listing responsibilities. No numbers, no hook, no CTA. |
| 3 | Reasonable narrative but leans on adjectives over results. Has a CTA but it's generic ("Feel free to connect"). |
| 4 | Clear problem → credibility → CTA structure. Includes specific results, reads like a human wrote it. |
| 5 | Opens with a hook that makes the target reader think "this is exactly my problem." Backs it with concrete evidence. Ends with a frictionless, specific CTA. |

## 4. Experience Section

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 1 | One or no entries. No descriptions. |
| 2 | Lists job titles only, or uses passive, responsibility-heavy descriptions ("Was responsible for..."). |
| 3 | Has bullet points with some action verbs. May have 1-2 numbers but they're vague ("improved efficiency"). |
| 4 | Each role has 2+ bullets with specific results using numbers. Action-oriented language. |
| 5 | Tells a career story — each role builds on the last, results are quantified and impressive, descriptions use precise numbers (not ranges like "20-30%"), recent roles are detailed while older ones are concise. |

## 5. Skills & Endorsements

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 1 | Fewer than 5 skills listed. Zero or near-zero endorsements. |
| 2 | Skills listed but top 3 are generic or misaligned with positioning. Few endorsements. |
| 3 | Top skills are relevant. Endorsements exist (10+) but from people in unrelated fields. |
| 4 | Top 3 skills are precisely aligned with positioning. Endorsements from recognizable/credible connections. Endorsement count inspires confidence. |
| 5 | Skills tell a coherent story about expertise. Top skill has 50+ endorsements from relevant professionals. Skills order is intentional (most important first). |

## 6. Activity & Content

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 1 | No activity visible. Last post more than 6 months ago. |
| 2 | Sporadic reposts with no commentary. Or only "I'm hiring" / job change announcements. |
| 3 | Occasional original posts (monthly). Content is relevant but not distinctive. Some comment engagement. |
| 4 | Regular posts (bi-weekly or more). Mix of original insights and curated content with commentary. Visible engagement from others. |
| 5 | Consistent, distinctive voice. Posts generate discussion. Content clearly serves the target audience. Profile visitor thinks "I want to follow this person." |

## 7. Network & Connections

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 1 | Fewer than 50 connections. |
| 2 | 50–200 connections, mostly from current/past companies. Narrow network. |
| 3 | 200–500 connections. Some diversity in industries/roles. |
| 4 | 500+ connections. Network spans relevant industries. Likely to have mutual connections with target audience. |
| 5 | 500+ connections with clear intentionality — the network reflects the user's professional positioning. Followers significantly exceed connections (thought leadership signal). |
